What Is NMGBC?

National Medical and General Biology Competition (NMGBC) is an annual scientific competition held by Asian Medical Student’s Association Universitas Indonesia (AMSA-UI). The event gathers students from all around Indonesia, where they compete in several different competitions, namely the general & medical biology quiz, as well as the public poster and social campaign competition. Each year, a specific theme regarding human health became the focus of the competition. Themes revolving around the cardiovascular system, digestive system, as well as special sensory system had been the focus of past NMGBCs, encouraging us to bring up a novel theme to NMGBC 2021.

This year, we have decided to raise the theme: Infectious Diseases, as we strongly believe that it is an issue that has remained relevant for decades, and is still a major burden for the economy & healthcare of our beloved country.

Various study results illustrate how Indonesia's diverse & rich ecosystem may sometimes bring harm to us, as it allows various diseases such as dengue, malaria, influenza to run rampantly in certain parts of the country. A 10-year study of our country regarding the various losses afflicted by the dengue virus has shown that there is little to no advancement in the reduction of losses, yet the number of new cases continues to rise each year. With these concerns in mind, we hoped that conceptualizing this year’s events around this topic would help in increasing participant’s knowledge and awareness of the various issues within the theme.

BIOLOGY COMPETITION


  • Preliminary Round

    Behold, the first round of this awaited occasion, Endurance test! All participants will be served two sets of questions, General Biology and Medical Biology, where the top 50 teams will be selected to proceed to the next round!

  • Semifinal: Integrative Exam

    Diving deep into the lives of FKUI students, we present to you a set of integrated practical questions, where each question are limited by time! Keep your eyes open and spirits high! Top 20 teams will proceed to the next round!

  • Final: General and Medical Biology Quiz

    Think you have what it takes to outwit fellow competitors? Challenge your biology know-how to the limit through our famous quiz! 5 teams will advance to the Grand Final!

  • Grand Final: Students Objective Oral Case Analysis (SOOCA)

    Can’t wait to experience the very lives of senior medical students? Have a shot at solving various realistic clinical cases in front of renowned consultants from FKUI-RSCM to bring home the National Medical & General Biology Competition 2021 title! All teams that reached this stage will bring home a very special prize!

PUBLIC POSTER


Re-introducing, our very own, public poster competition! This year, we invite participants to not only produce creative posters but also participate in a social campaign. Calling out all critical, innovative, and solutive youths to tackle one of Indonesia’s biggest healthcare challenges through your very own creations!


Preliminary round (online scoring) --> semifinal (poster presentation and voting) --> final (online campaign presentation)

  • Preliminary Round

    After the registration is complete, your poster and abstract will be scored and the top 15 teams will proceed to the semifinals to present their works!

  • Campaign

    This is where your team will do a social campaign through online media while your poster starts to be voted through our exhibition on NMGBC 2020’s Instagram!

  • Presentation

    Congratulations! Your works have been recognized as top-tier creations which are considered as successfully tackling Indonesia’s healthcare challenges! Prepare to showcase your works in front of panelists consisting of experts from all fields of healthcare, and bring home the title!

GENERAL REQUIREMENTS

- Participants are active senior high school students with proof of student ID card. - Each team must only consist of maximum of 2 students for Biology Competition and 3 students for the Public Poster competition. - Students from each team must come from the same senior high school. - Each student competing in the Biology Competition must prepare two devices for Zoom. - Each student competing in the Public Poster Competition must prepare one device for Zoom.
